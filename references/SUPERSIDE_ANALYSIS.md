# Superside.com - Deep Competitive Analysis for moonb
**Last updated:** March 2026
**Purpose:** Product positioning and homepage section ideas for moonb website rebuild

---

## 1. COMPANY OVERVIEW

**Tagline:** "Your creative team's creative team"
**Positioning:** AI-powered creative-as-a-service (CaaS) for enterprise marketing teams
**Target audience:** Enterprise marketing teams, creative leaders, ambitious startups, mid-market companies
**Key stat they lead with:** 94% ROI over 3 years (Forrester TEI study)
**Clients:** ~400 subscription clients including Amazon, Shopify, Salesforce, Puma, LVMH, Reddit, Cisco, Twitch
**Rating:** 4.6/5 across 467+ reviews, 91% recommend

## 2. HOMEPAGE SECTIONS (Top to Bottom)

### Section 1: HERO
- **Headline:** "Your creative team's creative team"
- **Subtext:** Positions as AI-first design and creative support to crush quarterly goals without burnout
- **CTA:** "Book a demo" (primary), likely secondary CTA for watching a video or exploring services
- **What it communicates:** You already have a team -- we extend it. Not a replacement, an amplification.
- **moonb takeaway:** The "team's team" framing is brilliant -- positions as partner, not vendor. moonb could adapt: "Your brand's creative engine" or similar. The hero leads with IDENTITY (who we are to you), not features.

### Section 2: LOGO BAR / SOCIAL PROOF
- **Content:** Enterprise client logos (Amazon, Shopify, Salesforce, Puma, LVMH, Reddit, Cisco)
- **What it communicates:** Instant credibility. "If Amazon trusts us, you can too."
- **moonb takeaway:** moonb needs a strong logo bar. Even 6-8 recognizable logos creates massive trust. If moonb doesn't have enterprise logos, use "Trusted by 50+ brands" with mid-market logos.

### Section 3: AI-POWERED CREATIVE / KEY DIFFERENTIATOR
- **Headline:** "Human brilliance powered by AI"
- **Content:** Explains their AI-enhanced creative workflows -- 40+ AI workflows, 60% cost savings, 2x delivery speed
- **Stats highlighted:** 17,770+ design hours saved, $1.4M in design costs saved, 500+ AI projects delivered
- **What it communicates:** We're not just designers -- we're the future of creative production. AI makes us faster AND cheaper.
- **moonb takeaway:** This is Superside's BIG differentiator in 2026. If moonb uses AI tools, this is worth calling out. If not, moonb should counter-position: "Human craft, not AI assembly lines" or similar.

### Section 4: SERVICES OVERVIEW
- **Content:** Visual grid/cards showing service categories:
  - Ad Creative
  - Social Media Creative
  - Presentation Design
  - Illustration Design
  - Branding Services
  - eBooks & Report Design
  - Concept Creation
  - Print Design
  - Packaging & Merchandise
  - Video Production
  - Motion Design
  - Email Creation
  - Web Design
  - Design Systems
  - Product Design
  - Copywriting
  - AI Consulting
- **Interactive elements:** Likely tabs or filterable categories
- **CTA:** "Explore services" or links to individual service pages
- **What it communicates:** We do EVERYTHING creative. One subscription, one partner.
- **moonb takeaway:** Superside lists 17+ services. moonb should clearly show its service scope. Even if fewer services, present them confidently. Consider a filterable grid or tabbed interface.

### Section 5: HOW IT WORKS / PROCESS
- **Steps presented:**
  1. Discovery call to understand needs, team structure, creative scope
  2. Tailored deep-dive with work samples, platform demo, Q&A
  3. Choose subscription plan, sign contracts
  4. Onboarding kick-off session
  5. Submit first project through Superspace platform
  6. Creative Project Manager reviews brief, production starts (often same day)
  7. Review, feedback, iterate
- **Platform highlighted:** "Superspace" -- their proprietary project management platform
- **Key selling points:** Projects can start in as little as 12 hours, no limit on collaborators, integrates with Slack/Asana/Monday
- **moonb takeaway:** A clear "How it works" section is ESSENTIAL. 3-4 steps max for the homepage (Brief > Create > Review > Deliver). Superside's process section builds confidence that there's a system, not chaos.

### Section 6: CUSTOMER STORIES / CASE STUDIES
- **Format:** Featured case study cards with metrics
- **Notable examples:**
  - **Amazon:** 1,092 assets delivered across 20 projects in 3 months
  - **Shopify:** Single concept rolled out in 25 sizes x 7 languages = 4,375 assets
- **What it communicates:** We deliver at SCALE for the world's biggest brands. Not just pretty pictures -- measurable output.
- **moonb takeaway:** Case studies with NUMBERS are powerful. moonb should show: "X deliverables in Y weeks" type metrics. Even smaller projects can be framed with impressive numbers (e.g., "12 social templates across 4 platforms").

### Section 7: COMPARISON / WHY SUPERSIDE
- **Content:** Three-way comparison: Superside vs. Agencies vs. Freelancers
- **Key positioning:**
  - vs. Freelancers: Consistency, brand knowledge, no scheduling hassles, one platform
  - vs. Agencies: Faster timelines, predictable pricing (no surprise costs), scales with you
- **What it communicates:** We're the best of both worlds -- agency quality with subscription simplicity
- **moonb takeaway:** A comparison section is VERY effective for B2B. moonb could do: "moonb vs. Freelancers vs. Traditional Agencies" -- showing moonb as the sweet spot. This directly addresses the buyer's mental model.

### Section 8: ROI / RESULTS METRICS
- **Stats featured:**
  - 94% ROI over 3 years (Forrester study)
  - $1.2M internal labor savings
  - $2.01M Net Present Value
  - Payback in under 6 months
  - $300K+ saved in first 200 projects
  - 60% faster project completion
- **Source credibility:** Forrester Total Economic Impact study (third-party validation)
- **moonb takeaway:** Third-party ROI validation is premium enterprise play. moonb can still show ROI: "Save X hours/month vs managing freelancers" or "Y% faster than traditional agency timelines." Even without Forrester, internal metrics work.

### Section 9: TESTIMONIALS / REVIEWS
- **Format:** Customer quotes with names, titles, company logos
- **Rating highlighted:** 4.6/5 across 467+ reviews
- **What it communicates:** Real people at real companies vouch for us
- **moonb takeaway:** Video testimonials or quote cards with headshots and titles. The company logo next to the testimonial adds credibility. Aggregate rating (if available from G2, Clutch, etc.) should be shown.

### Section 10: RESOURCES / BLOG PREVIEW
- **Content:** Links to learning center, blog posts, guides, reports, video library, playbooks
- **Categories:** Events & Summits, Guides, Reports, Video Library, Playbooks, Blog, Customer Stories
- **What it communicates:** We're thought leaders, not just service providers
- **moonb takeaway:** A blog/resources preview section shows expertise. Even 3 recent blog post cards add credibility. Topics should be about the INDUSTRY (creative production, brand building) not about moonb itself.

### Section 11: CTA SECTION (Bottom)
- **CTA:** "Book a demo" (repeated)
- **Supporting copy:** Reinforces the value proposition one final time
- **moonb takeaway:** Always end with a strong CTA. Superside uses "Book a demo" everywhere. moonb could use "Start a project" or "Book a call" -- action-oriented, low commitment.

## 4. PRICING PAGE ANALYSIS

### Pricing Model
- **Type:** Subscription-based with credits system
- **Billing:** Monthly subscriptions, annual commitment required
- **Currency:** USD

### Three Service Packages:

#### 1. Design Essentials
- **Level 1:** $5,000/mo for ~50 credits
- **Level 2-3:** Scaling up
- **Level 4:** $13,000+/mo for 150+ credits
- **Includes:** Core design services (ads, social, presentations, etc.)

#### 2. Digital Advertising
- **Starting at:** $7,500/mo
- **Includes:** Ad creative, campaign assets, performance-focused design

#### 3. End-to-End Creative
- **Starting at:** $9,000/mo
- **Includes:** Strategy + design + video + motion + AI consulting

### Credit System
- 1 credit = approximately 1-1.5 hours of work
- Unused credits roll over for up to 3 months
- AI-enhanced projects save ~60% of credits

### Key Pricing Page Elements
- Tiered structure within each package
- "Book a demo" CTA on every tier
- FAQ section addressing common concerns
- No self-serve signup -- everything goes through sales

### moonb takeaway:
- Superside starts at $5K/mo minimum -- this is enterprise pricing. If moonb is more accessible (e.g., $3K-$8K/mo range), that's a competitive advantage.
- The credit system adds flexibility but also complexity. moonb could counter with simpler pricing: "One plan. Unlimited requests. Fixed monthly fee."
- Superside doesn't show pricing publicly for enterprise tiers -- uses "Book a demo" as gating. moonb should consider whether transparent pricing (showing actual numbers) is a differentiator.
- The three-package structure (Design / Ads / Full Creative) helps buyers self-select. moonb could adapt this if it offers different service tiers.

## 7. KEY QUESTIONS ANSWERED

### What sections does Superside have that moonb might not?
1. **AI-Powered Creative section** -- Dedicated section explaining AI workflows and cost savings
2. **Comparison section** (vs. Agencies / vs. Freelancers) -- Directly addresses buyer objections
3. **ROI/Results metrics section** -- Hard numbers backed by third-party research
4. **Process/How it works** -- Step-by-step explanation of the engagement model
5. **Platform feature section** (Superspace) -- Highlights their proprietary tool
6. **Resource hub / Blog preview** -- Thought leadership content
7. **Customer stories with scale metrics** -- Not just "we did good work" but "we did THIS MUCH good work"

### How do they handle audience targeting?
- Primary positioning: "purpose-built for ambitious enterprise marketing teams and creative leaders"
- Secondary: Startups, mid-market, agencies
- Key pain point addressed: 78% of in-house creative teams can't keep up with demand; 76% of creative leaders experiencing burnout
- They DON'T have a "Made for" section with tabs by industry -- they target by ROLE (marketing teams, creative leaders) not industry vertical

### How do they show their work/portfolio?
- Dedicated "/our-work" page
- Case study cards with client logo, project description, and key metrics
- Individual customer story pages with deep-dive details
- Work samples on individual service pages
- Not a traditional gallery -- more results-focused than visual portfolio

### How do they explain their process?
- Step-by-step onboarding flow (Discovery > Deep-dive > Contract > Onboarding > Submit > Produce > Review)
- Emphasis on speed: "projects can start in as little as 12 hours"
- Platform-centric: Superspace handles briefs, revisions, feedback
- People-centric: Dedicated Creative Project Manager + Creative Director access
- Integration mentions: Slack, Asana, Monday.com

### How do they handle pricing on homepage vs. separate page?
- **Homepage:** NO pricing shown. Uses "Book a demo" CTA throughout.
- **Pricing page:** Shows three packages with starting prices, credit tiers, and "Book a demo" for details
- **Strategy:** Keep pricing curiosity high on homepage, reveal structure (but not full detail) on pricing page, gate enterprise pricing behind demo

### Do they have a blog preview section?
- Yes, resources/blog section on homepage
- Extensive blog covering: AI in creative, industry trends, best practices
- Also: Guides, Reports, Video Library, Playbooks, Events & Summits
- Acts as content marketing engine and SEO play

### Any unique sections?
1. **Forrester TEI Study callout** -- Third-party ROI validation (very rare in creative services)
2. **Superads platform** -- Separate AI-powered analytics tool for campaign ROI
3. **AI Hub** -- Dedicated content center for AI in creative
4. **Compare pages** -- Dedicated vs. Agencies, vs. Freelancers, vs. specific competitors
5. **Group demo option** -- "Join our Superside group demo" for lower-commitment entry point

## 8. STRATEGIC TAKEAWAYS FOR MOONB

### Sections moonb should consider adding:
1. **Comparison table** -- moonb vs. Freelancers vs. Agencies (high impact, addresses objections)
2. **Process/How it works** -- 3-4 step visual flow
3. **Results/metrics section** -- Even simple stats: "X projects delivered, Y happy clients, Z average turnaround"
4. **Case studies with numbers** -- Not just pretty work, but business outcomes
5. **Blog/resources preview** -- 3 recent posts to show thought leadership
6. **Audience targeting** -- Address who moonb is for (by role/team size, not just industry)

### Positioning opportunities vs. Superside:
1. **Price accessibility** -- If moonb is under $5K/mo, that's a HUGE differentiator
2. **Simplicity** -- Superside's credit system and tiered packages are complex. "Simple, flat monthly fee" wins
3. **Human-first** -- Counter Superside's AI-heavy messaging with premium human craft (if applicable)
4. **Founder-led / boutique feel** -- Superside is a 700+ person company. moonb can be more personal, more relationship-driven
5. **Transparent pricing** -- If moonb shows real prices on the website, that's trust-building vs. Superside's "Book a demo" gating
6. **Speed of starting** -- Superside requires discovery calls, deep-dives, contracts. If moonb can start faster, that's a win

### What Superside does well that moonb should learn from:
1. **Consistent CTA** -- "Book a demo" appears everywhere, no confusion
2. **Stats everywhere** -- Numbers build credibility (hours saved, assets delivered, ROI %)
3. **Comparison positioning** -- Directly addressing "why not an agency?" and "why not freelancers?"
4. **Pain point marketing** -- Leading with buyer problems (burnout, can't keep up, too slow)
5. **Layered social proof** -- Logos + testimonials + case studies + third-party studies
6. **Service breadth communication** -- Making it clear they handle everything creative
7. **Enterprise credibility** -- Name-dropping major clients at every opportunity
